My Inspirations

dndbeyond.com

One of the best websites for D&D themes, the official D&D beyond online character creator and manager. The website started out as a fan based online supplementary tool, granting access to many of the core D&D books. However, it was acquired back in 2022 by Wizards of the Coast, and is now the primary online resource digital items.

The website is built on a dark scale background, with red highlights and gold accents (left over from D&D's 50 year anniversary celebration). The site is built around link, with every feature of a D&D game (such as characters campaigns, magic items, monsters, dice, and so on) having it's own link somewhere on the page.

Notable Aspects

A must have of the internet, dark colours are easier on reader's eyes. D&D beyond's colour scheme evidently follows the same idea, with both the dark background, buttons, and nav bar, as well as the dimly coloured red highlights.

The D&D beyond website likes to hid their buttons and interactive elements inside their website through images. The design is immediately intuitive for users, but still helps to combine advertising and functionality into a compact web format.

Beyond the physical capabilities of the images in D&D beyond, they're also professionally made. Nothing sparks imagination like a well-made image, and nothing convinces customers to buy like a well-placed advert.
Space Unjammed

While not a website itself, Space Unjammed demonstrates plenty of design principles required in web based design. Space Unjammed in a homebrew (fan-made) resource created with the intent of improving the already existing mechanics of Spelljammer combat. Space-Unjammed is created by Valentin "Trekiros" Prevost.

Space Unjammed follows the regular style of other homebrew resources, vertically tall with a silver-gray background, filled with custom made images, smooth dark typography, and well formatted content.

Best Aspects

While D&D Beyond used professional artist to create their images, Space Unjammed creates it's own art on a subject by subject bases, allowing these images to more directly relate to the textual content.

Touched on earlier, the typography of Space Unjammed in smooth and dark, with black text being easy on the eyes and serifs to add a sense of professionalism to the rules provided.

Space Unjammed easily formats it's content into a flow like state, placing the first and foremost vital rules before the items/vehicles effected by said rules. Which this is to initially help with a reader's first experience with the resource, it also pushes the more important information closer to the start, and thus easier to refer to.

Sitemap

The sitemap for this website is split into 3 easy sections, with a consistent header and footer between each.

The Home Page (made out of the index) contains 4 sections of content, those being the Video and a quick About Me text, a segment on the Benefits of D&D, Applications of said D&D benefits in a realistic workplace, and some careers based on D&D.

The second page with a page containing my Resume, as well as a longer 'About Me' section, and a Contacts page. The 3rd and final page is the planning page, which is actually the one you're reading right now :D

The last 2 items are the header and footer. The header has a link to each of the 3 core pages, whereas the footer has information on the website author and a link to download the PDF of the website.
